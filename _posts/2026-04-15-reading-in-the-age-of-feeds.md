The feed is not neutral. Every scroll is a small surrender.

Not a moral surrender — a cognitive one. The feed conditions you to expect
resolution in three seconds: a punchline, a hot take, a dopamine tick.
Long-form reading asks for something different. It asks you to hold ambiguity,
to let an argument build, to sit with a paragraph that doesn't pay off for two pages.

## The Attention Economy Is Not a Metaphor

When economists talk about attention as a scarce resource, they mean it literally.
Every minute you spend inside an algorithmic feed is a minute your capacity for
sustained thought is being *shaped* by interruption.

The antidote isn't discipline. It's architecture.

## Build a Reading Environment

The most effective readers we know don't rely on willpower. They arrange their
environments so that the difficult thing — reading — is the path of least resistance.

A dedicated device. A physical book. A timer. These are not romantic affectations.
They are friction against distraction.
